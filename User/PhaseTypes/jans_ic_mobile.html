Before starting the study, please read the following information. If you have any questions, you may email the researchers above before starting. Once you have read the information sheet, you will be asked to complete a short consent form to confirm you are happy to take part.


What will I be asked to do?
The study takes around 45 minutes to complete using an internet connected device. We would ask that you try to complete the study in one go, but you need to complete pause and resume at a later point, that fine. As long as you do not clear your browsing history, you will be able to shut the browser window, re-open the study, and pick up where you left off. Procedurally, you will first answer some basic demographic questions along with two questions about how religious you consider yourself and how out of control you feel when saying a swear word out loud in response to pain and/or frustration. You will then rate 120 different words on 6 separate rating scales, providing a score for each scale from 1 to 9 by clicking on the appropriate response number based on your opinion. You will also be required to say whether you consider the word you are rating is a swear word or not. The words will be presented in four blocks of 30 words, and you will be allowed a break between each block should you wish. Please note, that within the 120 words you must rate, 83 are ‘swear words’ by which we mean they may be considered socially taboo and/or offensive (however, none are racial or ethnic slurs). After the making the ratings, you will then complete three questionnaires measuring different personality traits. Once you have completed the experimental procedure (just outlined) you will be debriefed to the study’s aims. We are collecting basic demographic information to monitor the diversity and inclusivity of those who take part in this study, as one of its aims is to increase the generalisability of our knowledge about how different people perceive different types of words.

Who can participate in this study and are there any benefits to taking part?
You can participate in this study if you are over 18 years old. Please do not complete this study if you are under 18 years old. All participants who take part in the study will be entered into a draw to win one-of-three £75 Amazon gift vouchers.

University of Reading psychology students can, however, swap their entry into the draw for SONA credits. Students can earn 1 credit upon completion of the study, however, if you wish to gain SONA credits from the study you must signup via SONA before taking part.

Are there any risks or disadvantages to taking part?
This study is investigating how people perceive different swear words across several different scales and how this may be influenced by our demographic differences. As a result, the procedure does involve reading and thinking about 83 words that can be considered swear words. This list of 83 swearwords, however, does not include any ethnic or racial slurs.

Due to the need to display each word on screen while you make your ratings, we would advise that you complete the study alone, in a quiet, private space. Furthermore, if you are uncomfortable with swearing or swear words in general, we would advise you do not take part in the study.

What will happen to my data/how will it be protected?
Your data will be kept anonymous, confidential, and securely stored, with only a random ID identifying it. The final dataset collected from this study will be preserved and made available in anonymised form, so that data can be re-used by others. This dataset will contain the descriptive statistics (means and standard deviations) for each word across the six rating scales, as well as the total individual difference questionnaire scores and demographic information of each participant. No personally identifiable information will be contained within this dataset.

Who can I contact about data privacy and storage?
The organisation responsible for protection of your personal information is the University of Reading (the Data Controller). Queries regarding data protection and your rights should be directed to the University Data Protection Officer at imps@reading.ac.uk, or in writing to: University of Reading, Information Management & Policy Services, Whiteknights House, Pepper Lane, Whiteknights, Reading , RG6 6UR.

The University of Reading collects, analyses, uses, shares, and retains personal data for the purposes of research in the public interest. Under data protection law we are required to inform you that this use of the personal data we may hold about you is on the lawful basis of being a public task in the public interest and where it is necessary for scientific or historical research purposes. If you withdraw from a research study, which processes your personal data, dependant on the stage of withdrawal, we may still rely on this lawful basis to continue using your data if your withdrawal would be of significant detriment to the research study aims. We will always have in place appropriate safeguards to protect your personal data.

As part of consenting to take part in this study, you will be asked if you are happy to be contacted about future research. This is entirely optional; you can still take part in the study even if you do not wish to be contacted in the future about other studies. Additionally, if you agree to be contacted but later change your mind, you can be removed from the list by emailing the study supervisor – Prof. Philip Beaman (c.p.beaman@reading.ac.uk).

You have certain rights under data protection law which are: (1) To withdraw your consent, for example if you opted in to be added to a participant register; (2) To access your personal data or ask for a copy; (3) To rectify inaccuracies in personal data that we hold about you; (4) To be forgotten, that is your details to be removed from systems that we use to process your personal data; (5) To restrict uses of your data; (6) To object to uses of your data, for example retention after you have withdrawn from a study.

Some restrictions apply to the above rights where data is collected and used for research purposes. You can find out more about your rights on the website of the Information Commissioners Office (ICO) at https://ico.org.uk. You also have a right to complain the ICO if you are unhappy with how your data has been handled. Please contact the University Data Protection Officer in the first instance.
