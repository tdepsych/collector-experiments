We would be grateful if you could assist us by participating in our online study
‘{{text}}’. Online disinhibition is the psychological phenomenon whereby increased anonymity in online interactions reduces the level of accountability felt. It is suggested that this phenomenon may explain why behavioural effects of swearing (e.g., an increase in pain tolerance and physical strength) do not always replicate using online testing methodologies. We are, therefore, interested in understanding how methodological changes may alter levels of online disinhibition, altering any subsequent behavioural effect of vocalising a swearword.

What will I be asked to do?
The study comprises three testing sessions, each taking approximately 15 minutes. Each session is online and uses the same procedure – the only difference is that in one session you’ll be asked to make eye-contact with a video showing the whole researcher, in another, a video showing just his eyes, and in a thrid, just a static image of his eyes, whilst completing a vocalisation task.

Procedurally, you will be asked to repeat either a swearword (Fuck) or a non-swearword (Flat) out loud, at your normal speaking volume and rate, for 20 seconds. After this, you will undertake a computerised ‘balloon inflation’ task where you score as many points as possible by pumping up an onscreen balloon without it bursting. Upon completion of the balloon inflation task, you will complete a lexical decision task, responding to whether a displayed word rhymes with the word you vocalised. You will then provide ratings of how offensive the vocalised word is to you and how humorous you found repeating it. Next you will complete a self-report emotional questionnaire. After this, you will be given a short break in which you can solve a wordsearch puzzle. You will then vocalise your second word before completing a second balloon inflation task. We are also collecting basic demographic information to monitor the diversity and inclusivity of those who take part but this is collected through the first 'Pre-Screen' session you completed after signing up for the study.

Who can participate in this study?
You can participate in this study if you are over 18 years old and a native English speaker. This is defined as someone born in, or having moved to before the age of 2 years old, a country where English is the first language. If you do not meet either of these requirements, please withdraw from the study before starting. If you are a University of Reading psychology student, recruited through the SONA recruitment system, you can earn 0.75 credits upon completion of the study.

Are there any risks or disadvantages to taking part?
This study is investigating how repeating a swearword may affect someone’s subsequent behaviour. As a result, the procedure does involve repeating the swearword ‘Fuck’ for 20 seconds in each testing session. the word search tasks (outlined earlier) include the use of written swear words. The swear word has been chosen to ensure it can illicit any required effect without being overly offensive. No other swearword is used or displayed as part of this study.

Due to the need to repeat the word ‘Fuck’ out loud during all testing sessions, we would advise that you complete before sessions alone, in a quiet, private space. Also, if you are uncomfortable with repeating the word ‘Fuck’ out loud, would advise you not to take part.

What will happen to my data/how will it be protected?
Your data will be kept anonymous, confidential, and securely stored, with only a random ID identifying it. The final dataset collected from this study will be preserved and made available in anonymised form, so that data can be re-used by others. This dataset will contain the mean number of pumps on unexploded balloons and demographic information of each participant. No identifiable information will be contained within this dataset.
